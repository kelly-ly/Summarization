a lithuanian gangster accused of two murders in his home country has cost taxpayers hundreds of thousands of pounds in a deportation farce after claiming he was too depressed to leave britain . kestutis martuzevicius repeatedly stalled efforts to extradite him , claiming they breached his human rights . in lithuania he is accused of 22 crimes including two murders , robbery and extortion between 1993 and 1997 , when he was a member of the feared daktarai gang . michael ellis , a former tory mp , said : ‘ this is a shocking example of labour’s human rights act putting the rights of suspected foreign criminals ahead of the rights of the british public . ’
