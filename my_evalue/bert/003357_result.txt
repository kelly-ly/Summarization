incredible satellite images have shown a massive sandstorm almost as large as the united states billowing across the arabian peninsula . according to the bbc , strong winds aggravated the problem , causing a ' near complete lack of vision in daylight ' , the uae national centre for meteorology and seismology said . despite sandstorms being a relatively common occurrence in that part of the world , they are not generally so big and rarely wreak so much havoc . in this instance , the storm also blew across saudi arabia 's empty quarter - the world 's second largest desert which reaches into yemen and oman .
