it is hard to believe that the mansion you see before you , with its bronzed clock tower and cherry wood doors , was initially a garage and chauffeur 's residence that would have been home to a rolls royce , or two . the converted four-bedroom home on lawrenny court , with its bronzed clock tower and cherry wood doors , was built as a garage to service the generous 57-room mansion homeden . the original downstairs garage area is made up of two distinct living areas , a well-equipped galley kitchen cleverly divided behind original garage doors that tuck away a fourth bedroom , bathroom , separate laundry and powder room .
