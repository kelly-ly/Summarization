japanese photographer uruma takezawa snaps a boy wears traditional tribal make up in the the lower omo river valley , ethiopia . born in 1977 , the japanese artist first began his career as a marine photographer , travelling the world to capture breathtaking underwater vistas . the wodaabe tribe are a nomadic people of cattle-herders and traders populating the sahel desert of west africa , in particular niger . from april 21 to may 5 , his show , land : photographs by uruma takezawa , will be on display at the foto-care gallery in new york city .
