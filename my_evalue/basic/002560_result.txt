a set of stone flakes shaped more than 3.3 million years ago could be the world 's oldest tools created by early human ancestors , according to researchers . the discovery could fundamentally change the current view of human evolution as - if correct - the date of the stone tools is 700,000 years older than any others found previously . it means that ancient human ancestors were creating tools hundreds of thousands of years before the appearance of the first ' man ' - the start of our branch of the evolutionary tree known as homo .
