an uber driver who was credited with stopping a potential mass shooting in chicago at the weekend by pulling out his own personal firearm and shooting the would-be gunman will not face any charges for his actions , a court has decided . court records say the man , identified by the washington post as everardo custodio , shot at people walking in front of the driver’s vehicle on friday night in the busy neighborhood . the driver - a 47-year-old man from the little italy neighborhood - then grabbed his own weapon and fired six rounds at custodio , striking him multiple times . he’s facing charges of aggravated battery with a firearm and illegal possession of a firearm .
