pregnant women who suffer extreme morning sickness are at greater risk of their children having language and speech delays , and attention disorders , warn researchers . about 10,000 women a year in the uk are affected by the debilitating hyperemesis gravidarum ( hg ) - a severe form of morning sickness which causes constant vomiting , crippling weakness and dehydration . pregnant women with hyperemesis gravidarum - the severe morning sickness suffered by kate middleton - are more likely to have children with language and speech delays , and attention disorders , a study claims .
