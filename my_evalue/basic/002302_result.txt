many teenagers are tempted to try e-cigarettes , but few adopt the electronic devices as habit , a new study has found . experts at cardiff university found use of e-cigarettes at least once was more common than having smoked a conventional cigarette in all age groups , bar the oldest ( 15 to 16 year olds ) . the proportion of teens who used e-cigarettes , but who had never smoked , rose from 5 per cent among 10 to 11 year olds , to eight per cent among 15 to 16 year olds .
