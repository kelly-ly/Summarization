britain 's biggest purpose-built air raid shelter , which could hold 6,500 people , was so sophisticated it even had electric lighting and flush toilets . safe : a bunker lined with corrugated steel to protect against the possible effects of a nazi bombing raid during the second world war . an engineering survey showed that the space was in fact unable to become a car park - but the advent of war meant that it was needed as an air raid shelter . history : a photograph of the shelter as it appeared in the 1930s , around the time of the second world war 's outbreak .
