an urgent headcount has taken place at a thai temple that 's home to 147 tigers in the care of monks after a vet complained to authorities that three were missing . wat pha luang ta bua in thailand 's western kanchanaburi province - commonly known as ' the tiger temple ' - has long proved a hit among tourists who flock there to visit the monks and be photographed next to their huge feline pets . however , thai wildlife officials began a headcount on friday after allegations from a temple vet earlier this week who complained to police that three tigers had recently disappeared from the sanctuary , sparking fears they may have been sold on .
