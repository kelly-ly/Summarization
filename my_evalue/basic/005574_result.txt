the graphic above shows how a blood sample can be mixed with microbeads that bind to cancerous cells ( labelled a ) . when these are placed in a clip on module ( b ) that attaches to a iphone camera ( c ) the microbeads produce distinctive diffraction patterns when they clump around a diseased cell ( top right ) . when added to a tissue or blood sample , these microbeads bind to any cancerous cells in the sample , changing the way light passes through it .
