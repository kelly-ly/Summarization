as a result of the recent spikes in hiv and hepatitis c infections among injecting drug users in rural indiana and kentucky , the controversial topic of syringe exchange programs has come to the fore again . dozens of studies have demonstrated conclusively how effective syringe services programs have been in the fight against hiv and hepatitis c transmission among injection drug users by reducing the reuse and sharing of dirty syringes -- without increasing drug use . for states such as new york and washington -- early adopters of these interventions -- syringe services programs have played a crucial role in driving down hiv transmission among injecting drug users .
