boliva’s cal orcko paleontological site is the result of tectonic activity , forcing earth upwards and preserving the precious trackways so that dinosaur tracks are seen on a vertical ' wall ' ( pictured ) . the wall , which is the largest dinosaur trackway in the world , is approximately 390 feet ( 120 metres ) tall and features tracks made by at least eight species of dinosaurs , the daily beast reported . the attraction , at fancesa limestone quarry in sucre , bolivia ( left ) , comprises some 462 trails made up of 5,055 prints ( right ) – and frequent landslides reveal new ones , some of which belong to unknown species .
