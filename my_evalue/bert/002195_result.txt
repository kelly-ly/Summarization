the owner of a british seaside hotel says it is virus-free after 100 holidaymakers were struck down by what health officials have described as a norovirus outbreak . dozens of people who visited the cooden beach hotel earlier this month came down with vomiting , diarrhoea and other symptoms of the highly-contagious airborne bug . owner james kimber told mailonline travel that the source of the illness has not been identified , but many of those who fell ill attended a wedding at the 41-room hotel on 11 april . a sign posted on the door of the hotel , where guests enjoy sweeping views of the english channel , warned visitors about an ‘ airborne virus’ at the premises .
