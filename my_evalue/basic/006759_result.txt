women paid 62p an hour in mauritius to make ‘feminist’ t-shirts have been beaten by police during protests over pay and conditions at their ‘sweatshop’ factory . dozens of workers at the factory – exposed by the mail on sunday for its low wages and prison-like accommodation for women making the ‘ this is what a feminist looks like’ t-shirts – have been sacked and deported for staging what bosses called ‘ an illegal strike’ . at the end of a peaceful three-day protest , bangladeshi migrant workers – who produce clothes for whistles , topshop and next – were surrounded by officers who charged at them , hitting out with batons , before dragging the screaming women away .
