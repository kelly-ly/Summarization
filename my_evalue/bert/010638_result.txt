a flickering rainbow of colours came with the march solar storm that made the northern lights even more remarkable than usual , and italian photographer giovanna griffo was in iceland to capture them in all their glory . griffo , 42 , who is based in latina , led a group of her students to vik and hella in the south of iceland where they were fortunate enough to be blown away by what is said to have been the strongest geomagnetic storm in the current solar cycle . while giovanna takes and edits all manner of photographs , she has a particular affinity with the night sky - and not just the spectacular aurora borealis but locations in her own country too . a man shines a torch but his light his overshadowed by the stars above circeo national park in latina , italy .
