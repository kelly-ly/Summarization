a bolt of lightning has been spotted soaring from the top of a cloud before looping back on itself and striking the ground in australia . the ' looping lightning ' image was taken by scott murray and the bolt appears to shot out and up from the cloud before curving towards the earth . as they develop , air acts as an insulator between the positive and negative charges in the cloud , and between the cloud and the ground . upwards lightning is very rare - estimates suggest less than one per cent of lightning travels in an ‘upwards’ direction - and it is typically classified into three groups , based on peak altitude .
