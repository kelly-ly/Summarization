the baby boom generation is set to leave one last burden to its children and grandchildren – a wave of funeral debt . costs are going up , it found , partly for technical reasons . crematoria now need to cover costs for removing fillings from the teeth of the dead , for example , because they must remove mercury from the body before disposal . funeral firms are under growing costs pressures , not least because some families can not or will not pay their bills , and the fees for a church of england vicar to conduct the funeral went up in 2013 from £102 to £160 .
