( cnn ) anthony ray hinton is thankful to be free after nearly 30 years on alabama 's death row for murders he says he did n't commit . hinton was 29 at the time of the killings and had always maintained his innocence , said the equal justice initiative , a group that helped win his release . `` race , poverty , inadequate legal assistance , and prosecutorial indifference to innocence conspired to create a textbook example of injustice , '' bryan stevenson , the group 's executive director and hinton 's lead attorney , said of his african-american client . hinton was accompanied friday by two of his sisters , one of whom still lives in the birmingham area .
