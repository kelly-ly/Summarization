nasa has released the first close-up views of the north pole of dwarf planet ceres from the dawn spacecraft . and it will soon study the whole planet - including its mystery bright spots . the spacecraft is currently on the side of the planet that is mostly shrouded in darkness , but over the coming months it will see more of the world in light . it will be at an altitude of just 225 miles ( 360km ) above the surface - below the orbital height of the international space station around earth - allowing it to get high resolution images of the surface .
