a woman whose designer crocodile-skin handbag was seized by the uk border force has triumphed in a test case bid to get it back . representing herself , mrs smouha told the first-tier tribunal at the royal courts of justice that she had been told by hikiji that import formalities would be ' taken care of ' . ruling in mrs smouha 's favour , judge redston said the border force had failed to consider the individual facts of her case . the skin had not been smuggled into the uk in defiance of cites and mrs smouha had ' reasonably relied ' upon hikiji , whose duty it was to obtain an import permit .
