brain scans on a group of professional comedians has revealed differences in the way the brains of these ' super speakers ' work while talking . compared to the less silver tongued among us , the super speakers – which also included a barrister and a radio presenter – showed less activity in a key area of the brain involved articulating and comprehending speech . dr joe devlin , a neuroscientist at university college london , told the guardian : ' the fact that they show less activity there reflects the fact that they simply find speech less effortful and i suspect part of that has to do with practice .
