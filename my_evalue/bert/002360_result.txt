britain will still be running a budget deficit at the end of the decade despite plans to return the country to the black , the international monetary fund warned last night . the global watchdog said the state will continue to spend more on public services and other areas such as welfare and overseas aid than it raises in tax throughout the next parliament . ed miliband is already planning a slower pace of deficit reduction than the tories with experts warning he could be borrowing around £30billion a year by the end of the decade . speaking at the launch of the fiscal monitor in washington , mr gaspar said : ‘ countries that fail to build buffers in good times often find themselves without fiscal space to manage a crisis and that is very costly . ’
