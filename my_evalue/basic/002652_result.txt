iraqi forensic teams have described how they wept as they began to excavate 12 graves , believed to be the final resting place of as many as 1,700 soldiers killed in cold blood by isis last summer . the teams began the gruesome task of uncovering the bodies of the young army recruits of camp speicher , slaughtered by the sunni isis militants as they surged across northern iraq for the ' crime ' of being shi'ite . today , the first of the 12 mass graves on the banks of the tigris river began to give up their terrible secrets , days after islamic state 's fighters were driven from the city by iraqi forces and shi'ite paramilitaries .
