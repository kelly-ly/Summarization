labour leader harold wilson secretly lobbied the bbc to change the time of popular comedy steptoe and son on the night of the 1964 election because he feared working class voters would stay at home and watch the show instead of supporting his candidates . according to new archive footage held by the bbc , mr wilson went to the home of bbc director general sir hugh greene and told him the show could cost him 20 seats . the labour party was afraid that its supporters would stay at home and watch a repeat of steptoe and son featuring harry h corbett , left , and wilfrid brambell , right , instead of coming out to vote in 1964 .
