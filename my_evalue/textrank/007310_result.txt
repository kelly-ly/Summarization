a new poll by yougov ahead of charles and camilla 's 10th wedding anniversary reveals that 49 per cent think camilla should take the traditional title of the wife of a reigning king , while 35 per cent believe she should be given a lesser title out of respect to diana , princess of wales and 16 per cent were undecided . almost half of all britons believe camilla , duchess of cornwall , right , should become queen when prince charles accedes to the throne although 35 per cent believe she should be rewarded with a lesser title . most people ( 42 per cent ) said they had neither a positive or negative impression of camilla , with 31 per cent saying they had a positive one and 23 per cent a negative one .
