prostate cancer resistant to conventional treatment could be all but wiped out by a therapy that boosts the immune system , claim researchers . each year in the uk around 41,000 men are diagnosed with prostate cancer and 11,000 die from the disease . lead scientist shabnam shalapour , of the university of california at san diego , said : ‘ the presence of such b-cells in human prostate cancer calls for clinical testing of this novel therapeutic approach . ’ symptoms of prostate cancer include the need to urinate more frequently , especially at night , painful ejaculation and blood in the urine or semen .
